Digital Health & Addiction Research

Developing innovative digital solutions to address mental health challenges and addiction

About My Work

My research combines technology and mental health care, exploring how digital tools can improve treatment approaches in addiction science. I develop and test evidence-based interventions to reduce the burden of addiction and mental health issues, especially among vulnerable populations. By finding what actually works and why, I aim to create practical solutions with real impact.

Current Positions

Since 2021, I've been working as a Postdoc at the Swiss Research Institute for Public Health and Addiction (ISGF) in Zurich, where my research concentrates on evaluating digital interventions targeting addictive behaviors.

In 2024, I began serving as Co-Head of Research and Quality Assurance at the Centre for Child and Youth Forensics (ZKJF), University Hospital of Psychiatry Zurich, Switzerland. This role has expanded my research focus to include youth forensic mental health investigating the effectiveness of therapeutic approaches for young people in the justice system.

My Research Focus

Digital Health Interventions

At the Swiss Research Institute for Public Health and Addiction, I evaluate digital tools that help people change harmful behaviors and improve their wellbeing. I'm particularly interested in what makes these interventions effective and how we can keep users engaged over time. My work spans both substance use and behavioral dependencies, where I investigate what treatments work and why through clinical trials, systematic reviews, and meta-analyses. I'm fascinated by the complexities of polysubstance use and how digital approaches can complement traditional treatment approaches

Youth Forensic Mental Health

Since joining the Centre for Child and Youth Forensics in 2024, a significant portion of my research focuses on young people in forensic mental health settings. This challenging intersection of youth development, mental health, and legal contexts presents unique research questions that drive my work. I'm particularly interested in addressing addictive behaviors among justice-involved youth, which research shows is highly prevalent: approximately 84% of youth in juvenile facilities report having used drugs at some point and around 60% meet the criteria for a substance use disorder in the year prior to entering custody. Despite these high rates, only about 28% of detained adolescents have ever utilized treatment services. These statistics highlight the critical need for targeted interventions that address the complex relationship between substance use, mental health, and criminal justice involvement in this vulnerable population.

About Me

Nikolaos Boumparis holds a Psychology degree from the University of Crete and a Research Master's in Clinical and Developmental Psychopathology from Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam. His Ph.D. at Vrije Universiteit focused on digital mental health interventions.

Since 2021, Nikolaos has been a postdoctoral researcher at the Swiss Research Institute for Public Health and Addiction, conducting randomized controlled trials, systematic reviews, and meta-analyses in addiction research. In 2024, he joined the Centre for Child and Adolescent Forensics at the University Hospital of Psychiatry Zurich to conduct research in forensic mental health settings.
